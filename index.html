Seasonal Music Listening Patterns

Understanding How Seasons Shape Our Musical Choices

Music is deeply intertwined with our emotions, activities, and environment. This project explores how seasonal changes influence music listening patterns across different genres throughout the year.

Why This Matters

  • Music preferences shift with seasons, reflecting changes in mood, activities, and cultural moments throughout the year
  • Seasonal patterns reveal how weather, holidays, and life rhythms influence the genres people choose at different times
  • Understanding yearly trends uncovers when certain genres peak and how listening habits evolve across months

Research Questions

  • RQ1: How do overall listening volumes vary across seasons and throughout the year?
  • RQ2: Which genres dominate each season, and how do their relative popularities shift?
  • RQ3: What hierarchical patterns emerge when analyzing seasonal and genre distributions together?

Key References

  • Last.fm Dataset - Music Technology Group, UPF
  • MusicBrainz - Open Music Encyclopedia

DATA OVERVIEW

Dataset Source

The Last.fm-360K dataset contains real user listening histories collected from Last.fm, a music streaming and recommendation platform.

  • Source: Last.fm Dataset (360K users)

VISUALIZATION 1: TEMPORAL TRENDS

RQ1: How do overall listening volumes vary across seasons and throughout the year?

Key Findings

  • Peak Summer Listening: Music listening peaks in summer at ~44,000 plays per week, dropping to ~34,000 in winter months (30% increase)
  • Gradual Transitions: Listening volume gradually increases from January through July, then steadily decreases through December - showing smooth seasonal shifts
  • Pop Forms Stable Base: Pop (gold) remains consistently the largest single genre throughout the year, providing a stable base layer of ~8k-14k plays per week
  • All Genres Rise Together: All genre layers visibly broaden during summer months (May-August), showing that seasonal increases affect multiple genres simultaneously

Design Rationale

The stacked area chart excels at showing temporal trends with composition. Weekly granularity (52 data points) captures seasonal transitions smoothly while avoiding daily noise. The stacking reveals both absolute volumes (total height) and genre contributions (layer thickness) simultaneously. Consistent genre colors enable pattern recognition across all visualizations.

VISUALIZATION 2: SEASONAL COMPARISON

RQ2: Which genres dominate each season, and how do their relative popularities shift?

Key Findings

  • Summer Has Highest Volume: Summer shows the tallest bar at ~576k total plays, while Winter has the lowest at ~453k plays
  • Pop Dominates All Seasons: Pop (gold) is the largest segment in every season, representing roughly 20-25% of all plays
  • Electronic Shows Strongest Seasonality: Electronic music (purple) nearly doubles from winter (~35k) to summer (~68k), showing the most dramatic seasonal variation
  • Jazz/Classical Counter-Trend: These calmer genres show relatively higher proportions in winter, bucking the overall upward summer trend

Design Rationale

Stacked bars enable direct comparison between discrete seasonal periods while preserving genre composition. The four-bar layout makes cross-season differences immediately visible. Bar heights show absolute volumes while segment sizes reveal relative contributions. This complements Visualization 1 by aggregating weekly data into seasonal summaries.

VISUALIZATION 3: MONTHLY HEATMAP

RQ2: Detailed monthly patterns and genre-specific seasonal behaviors

Key Findings

  • Pop Peaks June-August: Pop shows the darkest red cells in summer months, indicating highest play counts (~12k-14k plays per month)
  • Electronic's Sharp Contrast: Electronic displays the most dramatic color shift - light yellow in winter (~3-4k) to deep orange in summer (~6-8k)
  • Jazz/Classical Winter Preference: These genres show their darkest colors in December-February, demonstrating clear counter-seasonal patterns
  • Gradual Month-to-Month Changes: Colors transition smoothly across months rather than jumping abruptly, confirming gradual preference shifts

Design Rationale

Heatmaps excel at revealing two-dimensional patterns through color intensity. The 10√ó12 grid (genres √ó months) provides finer temporal resolution than seasonal aggregations, revealing gradual transitions. The sequential color scale (yellow‚Üíorange‚Üíred) intuitively maps to increasing play counts. This unique view type complements the previous charts by showing all genre-month combinations simultaneously.

VISUALIZATION 4: HOLIDAY EFFECTS

How do special periods (holidays) affect listening patterns compared to regular times?

Key Findings

  • Regular Periods Dominate: Non-holiday "Regular" periods account for the vast majority of listening activity across all genres
  • Summer Holiday Surge: Summer holiday period (weeks 27-35) shows elevated listening across all genres, particularly Pop and Hip-Hop
  • Holiday Genre Preferences: Christmas/New Year period shows relatively higher Classical and Jazz listening compared to other holiday periods
  • Thanksgiving Stability: Thanksgiving shows more modest changes, suggesting travel/family time may reduce overall listening without shifting genre preferences dramatically

Design Rationale

Grouped bars enable comparison of the same genre across different time periods (regular vs. various holidays). Unlike stacked bars, grouping emphasizes within-genre comparisons across conditions. This addresses the professor's feedback about exploring special events and reduces redundancy by providing a completely different analytical perspective from seasonal aggregations.

CONCLUSIONS & RECOMMENDATIONS

Key Findings Summary

  • Strong Seasonal Patterns: Summer listening peaks 30% higher than winter, driven by weather and lifestyle changes, with gradual rather than abrupt transitions
  • Pop Dominates Year-Round: Pop consistently represents 20-25% of all plays across every season, confirmed across all five visualizations
  • Genre-Specific Seasonality: Electronic music shows the strongest seasonal variation (doubling in summer), while Jazz and Classical counter-trend with winter peaks
  • Holiday Effects Are Real: Special periods show distinct patterns, with summer holidays amplifying the seasonal peak and winter holidays favoring contemplative genres

Strategic Recommendations

  • For Streaming Platforms: Implement seasonal recommendation algorithms that boost upbeat genres in summer and contemplative genres in winter. Pre-load seasonal playlists 2-4 weeks before transitions to match gradual preference shifts.
  • For Artists & Labels: Time album releases strategically - electronic and pop artists should target late spring/early summer (April-May), while jazz and classical artists should consider fall/winter releases (October-November).
  • For Playlist Curators: Create season-specific playlists that blend dominant genres with emerging seasonal preferences. Update compositions monthly to reflect gradual taste evolution shown in the heatmap.
  • For Marketing Teams: Allocate advertising budgets seasonally, with increased spending on electronic/pop campaigns in Q2-Q3 and classical/jazz campaigns in Q4-Q1. Target holiday periods with genre-appropriate campaigns.

Future Research Directions

  • Geographic Analysis: Investigate whether seasonal patterns differ between northern/southern hemispheres or vary by climate zones
  • Demographic Factors: Analyze if age groups, genders, or cultural backgrounds show different seasonal music preferences
  • Activity Context: Explore connections between listening context (commuting, exercising, studying) and seasonal genre choices
  • Predictive Modeling: Develop machine learning models to forecast seasonal trends and identify emerging genre-season relationships
